Only ~36% of employees meet KPI targets, highlighting significant improvement opportunities.

🔍 Key Insights

  • Previous year rating is the strongest predictor of performance
  • Referral recruitment produces higher-performing employees (~43%)
  • Training quality improves KPI, while excessive training reduces efficiency
  • Department significantly impacts KPI performance
  • Performance declines with tenure, indicating engagement issues

📊 Statistical Analysis

  • Chi-square test confirms strong relationships between Department, Recruitment, and KPI (p < 0.001)
  • ANOVA shows Previous Rating is the most influential variable
  • Training score and experience significantly impact performance

💡 Business Impact

Employee performance is driven more by job-related factors than demographics. Improving recruitment strategy, training effectiveness, and employee engagement is key to boosting performance.

🚀 Recommendations

  • Prioritize referral hiring to improve workforce quality
  • Focus on training effectiveness instead of quantity
  • Implement continuous performance evaluation systems
  • Improve engagement for long-tenured employees
