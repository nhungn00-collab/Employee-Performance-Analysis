📌 Overview

This project analyzes employee performance data to identify key factors influencing KPI achievement. The goal is to support HR teams in improving workforce performance through data-driven decisions.

🔍 Key Insights

  • Previous year rating is the strongest predictor of performance, with KPI achievement increasing from ~13% to over 60%
  • Referral recruitment produces higher-performing employees (~43%) compared to other channels (~28%)
  • Training quality (scores) positively impacts performance, while excessive training reduces efficiency
  • Department significantly affects KPI, with R&D and Operations performing highest and Sales lowest
  • Performance declines with tenure, indicating potential disengagement among long-term employees

📊 Statistical Analysis

  • Chi-square test shows strong relationships between Department, Recruitment, and KPI (p < 0.001)
  • ANOVA confirms significant impact of Age, Experience, Training Score, and Previous Rating
  • Previous year rating is the most influential variable (highest statistical significance)

💡 Business Impact

The analysis highlights that employee performance is driven more by internal and job-related factors than demographic characteristics. Organizations can improve performance by focusing on effective recruitment strategies, optimizing training quality, and managing long-term employee engagement.

🚀 Recommendations

  • Prioritize referral hiring to improve workforce quality
  • Focus on training effectiveness rather than quantity
  • Implement continuous performance evaluation systems
  • Target low-performing departments with tailored strategies
  • Develop engagement programs for long-tenured employees
